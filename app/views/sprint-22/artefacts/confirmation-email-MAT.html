Alternatively, fill in the Register your interest form (opens in new tab) and complete the paperwork for any additional schools offline.

Objections

The new suppliers will start the process of switching your supply approximately one month before your new contract's start date. If the transfer from your current supplier is refused, this is referred to as an objection. DfE will notify you if there has been an objection and guide you on what to do next.

Interim rate

You may be placed on an interim rate to start with, depending on your current contract end date and the start of the next available V30 rate.

For example, if your current contract ends on 31 December 2025 and you're set to switch to the V30 rate on 1 April 2026, you'll be on an interim rate between 1 January 2026 and 31 March 2026.

We'll keep you informed of forecast unit rates once your contract starts by sending you a monthly email update.

Invoices and portal access

We'll also contact you with further instructions about submitting your VAT certificate and Direct Debit mandate, if you selected Direct Debit as your preferred payment method.

You'll be granted access to the new supplier(s) portal(s) on or shortly after your contract's start date. Portal access allows you to view your consumption and billing information and input meter readings.

Support

You can contact DfE by replying to this email if you need help with:

  • adding more schools or removing existing ones from your energy contract (only applicable to federations and multi-academy trusts)
  • contract-related questions, including guidance on termination notice periods and contractual obligations
  • escalating and resolving any persistent problems with your energy supplier
  • account configuration and ensuring correct billing information across all your sites
  • managing energy contracts during change of tenancy situations
  • renewable energy options
